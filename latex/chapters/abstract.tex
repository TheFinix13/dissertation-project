This dissertation investigates whether a deep reinforcement learning agent
that conditions on its own forecaster's predictive uncertainty can achieve
superior risk-adjusted returns compared to standard baselines. A Proximal
Policy Optimization (PPO) agent is augmented with an LSTM-based probabilistic
forecaster trained via Gaussian negative log-likelihood, injecting a real-time
uncertainty signal into the trading environment's state space. The agent is
evaluated on a market sample of 70 stocks over the 2022--2025 test
window against passive buy-and-hold, a manually-tuned trailing stop-loss, and
a baseline PPO with no uncertainty channel. Results show that the
uncertainty-aware agent reduces maximum drawdown on 100\% of tickers versus
buy-and-hold while maintaining competitive Sharpe ratios, demonstrating
effective capital preservation under regime stress.
